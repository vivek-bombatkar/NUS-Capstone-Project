Product Feedback Report - Q1

Overview:
Customer satisfaction scores dropped to 62% in Q1, down from 74% in Q4 of the previous year.
The primary driver of dissatisfaction was logistics and delivery performance.

Shipping and Delivery:
40% of orders exceeded the promised delivery timeline.
The average delay was 3.2 days beyond the expected date.
Customers in rural areas experienced the longest delays, averaging 5.1 days.
Third-party logistics partners were cited as the root cause in 78% of delay cases.

Product Quality:
15% of customers reported receiving items that did not match the product description.
Defect rates were highest in the electronics and kitchenware categories.
Return requests increased by 22% compared to the previous quarter.

Customer Sentiment:
Negative reviews mentioning delivery delays increased by 38% on the feedback portal.
Repeat customers showed a higher tolerance for delays but flagged quality issues more frequently.

Recommendations from Report:
1. Renegotiate SLAs with logistics partners.
2. Introduce real-time shipment tracking for customers.
3. Implement pre-dispatch quality checks for high-defect categories.
